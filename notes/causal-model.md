# Causal model

## Feedback loops

1. AI capability -> enterprise adoption -> labor restructuring -> political backlash -> procurement and liability constraints.
2. AI capability -> cyber/fraud scaling -> authentication and provenance markets -> institutional trust shifts.
3. AI capex -> chips/memory/power constraints -> market concentration -> geopolitical competition.
4. Synthetic media -> epistemic distrust -> trusted networks/provenance -> polarization around legitimacy.
5. AI tutors/companions -> family and school adaptation -> inequality and youth formation.
6. AI-mediated loneliness -> religious/spiritual/community response -> new norms around human presence.
7. War/geopolitics -> defense AI investment -> export controls -> chip competition -> further AI concentration.
8. Political instability -> institutional distrust -> private systems, religious community, platform mediation, verified networks.
9. Infrastructure constraint -> higher AI costs -> concentration of capability -> policy backlash and national-security intervention.
10. Labor compression -> apprenticeship breakdown -> elite training redesign -> class stratification.

## Actors with agency

Frontier labs, hyperscalers, memory suppliers, utilities, regulators, courts, insurers, militaries, intelligence services, campaigns, unions, school districts, religious institutions, local governments, cyber insurers, banks, platforms, parents, and criminal networks.

## Actors mostly acted upon

Junior workers, small municipalities, small businesses, low-income students, local newsrooms, fragile households, migrants, indebted states, and institutions without procurement or security capacity.
