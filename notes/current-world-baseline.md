# Current world baseline (evidence available by May 2026)

This project treats late summer 2026 as the scenario start, but it does not claim to know late-summer facts from May 2026. The baseline rests on current evidence: rapid AI capability improvement, broad enterprise experimentation, rising capex for AI data centers, tightening power and memory constraints, low institutional trust, a Trump second term, grinding war in Ukraine, European rearmament, Chinese pressure around Taiwan, Middle East volatility, and early but visible social disruption from AI companions, cheating, synthetic media, and fraud.

## AI and infrastructure

Public evidence supports a faster-takeoff baseline without assuming magic. AI systems are becoming useful machine labor in bounded workflows: coding, analysis, customer support, marketing, internal search, legal review, document production, translation, and administrative work. Agent reliability remains uneven, but the direction is toward longer autonomous task horizons. The physical stack matters: GPUs, HBM, DRAM, NAND, power, interconnection, cooling, advanced packaging, substations, permitting, and export controls constrain deployment and concentrate advantage in hyperscalers, frontier labs, and governments.

## Politics and geopolitics

The United States is polarized and institutionally strained. Trump governs through executive pressure, loyalist appointments, litigation, tariff and immigration fights, and conflict with universities, cities, media, courts, and parts of the civil service. The 2026 midterm environment is structurally difficult for the president's party, but the Senate map and fragmented media blunt the backlash.

Ukraine remains an attritional war. Russia is degraded but adaptive. Europe is rearming, but unevenly. China is economically pressured but strategically patient; Taiwan faces persistent gray-zone coercion. The Middle East remains a managed-instability system around Iran, Israel, Gaza, Gulf hedging, Turkey, drones, proxies, cyber operations, and energy. India rises as a swing power rather than a simple U.S. ally.

## Social baseline

Low trust, loneliness, family stress, school assessment breakdown, and religious searching precede the AI transition. AI does not create those conditions; it monetizes, amplifies, and reorganizes them. Families face cloned-voice scams and companion bots before they have norms. Schools face take-home assessment collapse before procurement catches up. Churches, mosques, synagogues, temples, libraries, clinics, unions, and local newsrooms become trust infrastructure where they remain credible.
