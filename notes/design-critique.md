# Design critique

1. Serious editorial product or generic AI page? The design uses warm off-white, charcoal, oxblood, serif display type, dense tables, restrained dividers, and no neon/glass. It reads as briefing/essay rather than SaaS.
2. Long essay pleasant to read? The prose column is controlled; year nav and progress bar support orientation.
3. Visual hierarchy clear? Landing hero, small-caps labels, large serif headings, table/card contrasts, and category chips establish hierarchy.
4. Timeline and forecasts useful? Timeline has filters; forecasts include baseline/rationale/alternative/signals.
5. Design supports argument? Yes: it privileges reading, causality, chronology, and inspectable assumptions.
6. Mobile good? Layout collapses to single column, sticky nav becomes horizontal scroll/jump menu, cards stack.
7. Ugly defaults? Replaced starter page, metadata, favicon mark, README.
8. Professional critique: could use custom illustrations or charts, but restraint is appropriate.
9. Polish: hover states, semantic structure, responsive CSS, progress bar, no placeholder text.
10. Credibility: sober typography and source/method pages establish seriousness before deep reading.
