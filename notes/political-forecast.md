# Political and geopolitical forecast

## United States

2026: Democrats gain the House; Republicans hold the Senate narrowly. Trump retains executive tools but loses legislative freedom. Investigations, shutdown fights, court confrontations, and agency battles define 2027.

2028: Gretchen Whitmer defeats JD Vance. The result is close but certified after litigation and state-level disputes. Democrats win by presenting competence, labor protection, abortion rights, anti-corruption, and AI-fraud safeguards. Republicans keep a large base but suffer from tariff/inflation fatigue, Trump exhaustion, and fear of dynastic MAGA.

2032: Tom Cotton defeats a Democratic successor coalition after the 2030 correction, disorder politics, labor anxiety, and cyber/fraud fatigue. The GOP campaign promises order, family protection, industrial strength, and hard power. It does not repeal AI; it nationalizes parts of the AI agenda.

## Ukraine and Russia

Ukraine avoids defeat but does not recover all territory. A frozen armed armistice emerges around 2028 after U.S. pressure, European compensation, and battlefield exhaustion. Russia remains sanctioned, militarized, and China-dependent, with a managed elite succession risk rather than democratic collapse.

## China/Taiwan

China increases gray-zone pressure and rehearses blockade/quarantine options. A 2029 maritime quarantine crisis shocks markets and accelerates semiconductor contingency planning but stops short of invasion. Taiwan remains autonomous but permanently more fortified and economically pressured.

## Europe

Europe rearms unevenly. Poland, Nordics, Baltics, and parts of Central Europe move fastest. Germany and France increase spending but face fiscal and political friction. EU AI regulation remains influential, but Europe depends on U.S. cloud, chips, and frontier models more than it wants to admit.

## Middle East

No regional settlement. Iran-Israel conflict remains episodic and dangerous. Gulf states hedge between U.S. security, China trade, India growth, and AI infrastructure ambitions. Drones, cyber, proxies, and energy-market shocks remain recurring features.

## India and Global South

India becomes indispensable but not aligned. It benefits from AI services, English-language technical talent, and China balancing, while struggling with education inequality, job displacement, religion/nationalism, and state-capacity variation. Africa and Latin America see uneven AI adoption through platforms, mining, agriculture, remittances, debt politics, and education tools.
