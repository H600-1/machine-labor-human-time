# Scenario model

## Scenario spine

Baseline: faster AI takeoff, no terminal calamity. AI becomes cheap, useful, partially reliable machine labor across software, administration, education, media, cybercrime, logistics, defense, research, and household management. The world avoids extinction, total nuclear war, permanent civilizational collapse, and a clean rogue-AI takeover. It does not avoid wars, democratic backsliding, cyber disruption, market crashes, religious backlash, labor shock, or institutional legitimacy crises.

## Baseline calls

- 2026 U.S. midterms: Democrats narrowly take the House; Republicans hold the Senate by a small margin. Trump loses legislative ease but retains executive power.
- 2028 U.S. presidential election: Democrat Gretchen Whitmer defeats Republican JD Vance after a legitimacy-disputed but ultimately certified election. The campaign turns on cost of living, corruption, labor anxiety, AI fraud, abortion/family policy, and exhaustion with Trumpism.
- 2032 U.S. presidential election: Republican Tom Cotton wins a narrow victory over the incumbent Democratic coalition's nominee after recession scars, cyber/fraud fatigue, and an order-and-family backlash. The governing coalition is nationalist, pro-defense, pro-industrial-policy, and selective about AI regulation.
- Ukraine: the front largely stabilizes in 2027-28 into an armed armistice without a full peace. Ukraine remains sovereign, battered, European-armed, and outside restored 1991 borders. Russia keeps Crimea and portions of occupied east/south, pays a sanctions and dependency cost, and remains dangerous.
- Russia: regime durability through Putin or a managed successor; no liberal revolution. Russia becomes a poorer, militarized, China-dependent spoiler state.
- China/Taiwan: repeated blockade rehearsals, cyber pressure, maritime quarantine crisis in 2029, no full invasion. Taiwan remains de facto autonomous but under higher permanent pressure.
- AI labor market: junior white-collar compression starts in software, support, marketing, legal operations, finance operations, and media; productivity gains are real but concentrated. Apprenticeship breaks before credentialing adapts.
- Economic shock: 2030 AI capex/valuation correction after a major agent-security scandal and overbuilt speculative data-center financing. It is severe but not 2008-level systemic collapse.
- Infrastructure: memory, power, interconnection, and advanced packaging bind through 2031; expansion partially catches up by 2034 but demand keeps pressure high.
- Cyber/fraud: AI-enabled impersonation, BEC, synthetic IDs, school/municipal ransomware, and political forgeries force passkeys, hardware keys, signed media, verified channels, liability shifts, and new household verification norms.
- Religion/social trajectory: loneliness plus synthetic intimacy produces both techno-spiritual movements and a traditional embodied-community revival. Religious institutions that offer real presence and disciplined AI use gain social importance.

## Drivers

1. Capability: longer agent task horizons, better tool use, better coding and analysis.
2. Deployment: enterprise workflows, household assistants, school tools, campaign operations, defense systems.
3. Infrastructure: chips, HBM, power, interconnection, cooling, data-center finance, fabs, export controls.
4. Politics: democratic stress, backlash, lawsuits, procurement, legitimacy, state capacity.
5. Geopolitics: Ukraine, Russia, Taiwan, export controls, defense AI, industrial policy.
6. Social meaning: loneliness, family administration, youth formation, religious discernment, trust.
7. Security: fraud, cybercrime, provenance, authentication, insurance, evidentiary standards.

## Stabilizers

Courts, insurers, procurement rules, professional liability, conservative enterprise buyers, power bottlenecks, audit requirements, religious and civic norms, passkeys/hardware keys, signed media, local trust networks, and the stubborn difficulty of changing institutions.

## Destabilizers

Cheap plausible content, cloned voices, agentic error cascades, labor-market compression, campaign delegitimation, authoritarian pressure, state-backed cyber operations, market concentration, infrastructure fights, youth companion dependence, and war-driven defense AI investment.
